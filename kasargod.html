Top Attractions in Kasargod
Majestic hills, pristine beaches, quiet backwaters, rich cultural heritage and unique art forms and festivals make Kasaragod - Kerala's northernmost district - a compelling tourism destination. Yes, Kasaragod is rising. The travel and tourism sector of the district is poised for exponential growth. For those who are looking to make an investment the sector in Kerala, Kasaragod is brimming with opportunities.
Ananthapura Lake Temple
Ananthapura Lake Temple is located in the middle of a lake in the village of Ananthapura. It is the only lake temple in Kerala and is known as the moolasthanam (the original abode) of Lord Padmanabha of Sree Padmanabhaswamy temple of Thiruvananthapuram.

Bekal Fort

Bekal Fort is a historical structure built in the 17th century and has itself become part of an identity of the district.The Bekal beach is only a kilometre away from the fort which offers an enticing view of the imposing fort.
Chandragiri Fort
Chandragiri Fort is another historical landmark in the district situated in the south-east of Kasaragod town.The fort is now maintained by the Archaeology Department of India. Boat services are available here that will take you to nearby islands and palm groves.
Kanhangad
Kanhangad is a populous municipality and the commercial town in Kasaragod district. It is a coastal town bounded by the municipal town of Kasaragod in the North, Nileshwaram the cultural epicentre of the district to its south, the hilly town of Pannathur to its east and the Arabian Sea to its west.
Ranipuram Hills
Ranipuram Hills is a popular scenic hotpot in Kasaragod district which is situated about 43 km away from Kanhangad. Formerly known as Madathumala, this picturesque stretch of hills stands at a height of 750 m above sea level and is the point of highest altitude in the entire district.
